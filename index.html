Background

I have one sibling. We were raised on a farm, 17 miles from the nearest town. We learned to work hard, eat right, and enjoy the simple things in life.

I did well in school and enjoyed many sports and hobbies growing up. I played many years of hockey, soccer, rugby, and other sports, and these activites were the most important things in my early life:

  • hiking

  • fishing

  • horseback riding

  • dirtbiking

  • drumming

  • downhill skiing

  • computer programming

And then as a young adult, I went straight from high school into college. I graduated from the College Of New Caledonia with a Business Administration diploma majoring in C.I.S. (Computer Information Systems). My first major decision in my life happened when I had to choose between going on to achieve my bachelor Computer Science degree at UBC (University Of British Columbia) or start working. A great opportunity presented itself to me, and so I chose working at my career as a computer programmer.

To this day, I am still a computer programmer.

I look for the good in all hardware, O/S, platforms, and programming languages, tools, and technologies. Just like humans: nobody is perfect; it is similar with computers: no one thing is the best thing for every job in the world. It is generally a good idea to use the best tool for the job.
